Marketing in a privacy-first world

Bring clarity in how you collect data

Privacy First

After years of invasive data collection, it’s clear: people want control over their data and respect for their privacy.

The GDPR and ePrivacy regulations specify that cookies can be read / written only after explicit consent from the user, this results in less data collected.

Talking about digital privacy might sounds as an oxymoron, you should find the right balance between respect for people and business objectives.

Privacy by design

Considering privacy by design in digital analytics is important:

  • it is a form of respect for its users but
  • and attention to data collection

It is easy to collect data, even easier it is to have others collect data (see Facebook), it is less easy to organize a thoughtful and measured data collection.

A first vital tool is the consent management platform (CMP) which manages the layout of the banners, the collection of consents, the management of tags according to the type of consent, thanks to a centralized platform.

The CMP solves the problem for the user but not completely for the company: enabling prior consent means losing the user’s measurement.

Machine Learning running for help

Google’s Consent Mode is the help piece for companies: through conversion models, consent mode can help estimate activities when traffic and conversions observation is not possible. The individual users observed in this traffic modeling are not identified and, moreover, are treated at an aggregate level.

How a project starts

A privacy project in digital analytics takes place in collaboration with the company DPO, who will provide the operating indications of the measurement and profiling stack.

The digital analyst will provide technical advice to implement the management of consents on digital platforms by relying on solid solutions such as Cookiebot or CookiePro.

Consent Mode advantages

Considering consent mode within a digital analytics and consent management project has a double advantage:

  • for the user, where his privacy is respected
  • for the company, which can have a campaign measurement even from blocked cookies
