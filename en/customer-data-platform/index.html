Customer Data Platform

A customer data platform (also known as CDP) is a service that combines customer data from multiple sources into a single work environment.

CDP, with its holistic approach, helps all teams in a digital project:

  • Development -> ETL hands over to CDP -> team focus on key functionalities
  • Marketing -> personas and segments in real time -> campaign optimization
  • Data & Analytics -> solidity in data collection -> unified analysis
  • Product -> reduction of the silo model -> focus on the development of experiences and functionalities

Being customer centric is a data problem

The centrality of the customer, in the digital world, is a data problem: both due to the tendency of platforms to think in silos, for physiological reasons of digital (to consider one the same user who uses both the app and the site, one must prepare the measurement) and for the sunset of third-party cookies.

Customer data platforms such as Segment adopt the concept of identity resolution to unify users’ online signals across all channels, including anonymous signals (i.e. without a user id) that are grouped into a child: parent relationship as soon as the identity is applied. resolution, a valid solution both in the B2C and in the B2B sector.

CDP, to solve the data problem, works on 4 pillars

  • Connect platforms with channels
  • Govern the data, classifying them and making them consistent
  • Synthesize data to create a source of truth
  • Activate the data in operation, synchronizing them in real time with marketing automation

How a project works

Like a digital analytics project, a customer data platform project begins with a first phase of gathering business requirements.

Clarifying the user’s journey means having solid data collection. For this reason, a consultancy and implementation project is outlined in several phases starting from the key events up to planning all the events, with a rigorous testing and verification process before going into production.

These events will be the basis of identity resolution.

The project is developed in several sprints which include:

  • Study and preparation of the measurement plan
  • Coding of key events in a development environment
  • Configuration of sources and development channels, as well as identity resolution, always in the development environment
  • Testing, Quality Assurance and push to production

Benefits of a CDP

Compared to a tracking project on Google Tag Manager, the initial investment on a Customer Data Platform as Segment is greater but brings a solid cross-channel measurement base and future proofs.

Once integrated it will be possible to:

  • Collect customer data and integrate your data stack in minutes
  • Automatically perform identity resolution and create unified customer profiles
  • Build personalised, omnichannel customer journeys based on your audiences

One of the key events in a user journey, for example, is the add to cart

Without segment:

  • DataLayer coding for Google Analytics
  • Setting up Google Analytics tags in Google Tag Manager
  • Event coding for the Facebook pixel
  • Event coding for each single platform used

With Segment

  • Coding of the event on the website
  • Enabling of destinations affected by UI

Differences between CDP and a TMS like Google Tag Manager

It is easy to confuse a Customer Data Platform for a tag management system like Google Tag Manager.

A CDP as a Segment is focused on measuring the user’s journey in every step, making the consistency between events, triggers and parameters its strength, as well as supporting various data warehouses.

Google Tag Manager is ideal for companies that have supported their marketing stack on the Google Marketing Platform, taking advantage of the perfect integration between GTM tags and Google products.
